/ How I Work

Why teams struggle with data

Most teams struggle not because they lack data, but because insights don't drive action. Experimentation programs stall without proper foundations. Analytics sit in dashboards while decisions get made on gut instinct. User behavior goes unanalyzed. The gap between "we have data" and "we act on data" kills velocity.

The systems I build

Whether through experimentation, behavioral analysis, or product insights — I build infrastructure that makes data actionable: AI removes bottlenecks (insight synthesis from 7 days to 20 minutes, automated reporting, natural language querying), analytics reveals patterns (user intent analysis, keyword mapping, funnel diagnostics), and frameworks ensure rigor (statistical guardrails, evidence-based prioritization, systematic decision-making). This is how you turn 300+ annual tests into compounding knowledge, or £500k revenue from a single behavioral insight.

What you get

You get systems where data drives decisions, not decorates them. Experimentation velocity becomes learning speed. Analytics uncovers revenue opportunities. And every insight compounds institutional knowledge instead of disappearing into spreadsheets. I've proven this across experimentation programs, product analytics, and AI infrastructure.
